Departments

Hospitals are complex organizations that consist of different departments, each with its own specific functions and roles. These departments work together to provide comprehensive medical care to patients.

  • Cardiology

    The cardiology department is responsible for diagnosing and treating heart-related diseases.

  • Neurology

    This a medical specialty that focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of diseases and disorders of the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, and nerves..

  • Hepatology

    Hepatology is a medical specialty that focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of liver diseases and disorders.

  • Pediatrics

    The pediatrics department is dedicated to providing medical care to infants, children, and adolescents.

Cardiology

Cardiology is a medical specialty that focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of diseases and disorders of the heart and blood vessels.

Cardiologists are specialized medical professionals who are trained to manage a wide range of heart-related conditions, such as coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, congenital heart defects, and valve disorders.

They use a variety of diagnostic tools and techniques, including electrocardiography (ECG), echocardiography, stress testing, cardiac catheterization, and angiography to diagnose and manage heart diseases. Treatment options may include medications, lifestyle changes, dietary modifications, and in some cases, cardiac procedures such as angioplasty, stenting, or heart surgery.

Neurology

Neurology is a medical specialty that focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of diseases and disorders of the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, and nerves.

Neurologists are specialized medical professionals who are trained to diagnose and treat a wide range of neurological conditions, such as Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, epilepsy, multiple sclerosis, stroke, and headaches. They use a variety of diagnostic tools and techniques, including imaging tests, neurological examinations, and electrophysiological studies, to diagnose and manage neurological diseases.

Treatment options may include medications, lifestyle changes, and rehabilitation therapies. Neurologists work closely with other healthcare professionals, such as neurosurgeons, neuropsychologists, and physical therapists, to provide comprehensive care for patients with neurological conditions.

Hepatology

Hepatology is a medical specialty that focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of liver diseases and disorders.

Hepatologists are specialized medical professionals who are trained to manage a wide range of liver-related conditions, including hepatitis B and C, cirrhosis, liver cancer, fatty liver disease, and autoimmune liver disease. Hepatologists also work closely with gastroenterologists, surgeons, radiologists, and other healthcare professionals to provide comprehensive care for patients with liver-related conditions.

They use a variety of diagnostic tools and techniques, such as blood tests, imaging studies, liver biopsy, and endoscopy, to diagnose and manage liver diseases. Treatment options may include medications, lifestyle changes, dietary modifications, and in some cases, liver transplantation.

Pediatrics

Pediatrics is a medical specialty that focuses on the health and well-being of infants, children, and adolescents.

Pediatricians are specialized medical professionals who are trained to manage a wide range of health issues that affect children, from minor illnesses to complex medical conditions. They provide preventive care, diagnose and treat acute and chronic illnesses, and monitor growth and development of children.

Pediatricians also provide immunizations, screenings, and counseling to help parents and children maintain healthy lifestyles. They work closely with other healthcare professionals, such as pediatric nurses, child psychologists, and pediatric surgeons, to provide comprehensive care for children. The goal of pediatrics is to promote the health, growth, and development of children from infancy through adolescence.
